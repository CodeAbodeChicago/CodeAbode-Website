About

We are a new Chicago-based code academy, designed to turn students into web and mobile app developers. We offer both ‘boot camp’ and part-time options. The bootcamp runs 14 weeks – 7 courses that all build on each other in increments of 2 weeks each. In the part time program, classes meet twice a week for 10 weeks – about twice as much class time as a university quarter course.


CodeAbode is a division of the National Museum of Health and Medicine Chicago (NMHM Chicago). The mission of the National Museum of Health and Medicine Chicago is to promote knowledge of health and medicine and to encourage scientific research and innovation. We aim to engage the public through cutting-edge technologies and digital exhibitions, and to serve as a locus of education for scholars, innovators, students, educators and the general public. As a part of the museum, CodeAbode's niche in the marketplace will be our focus on health, medicine and fitness applications. Students do not need to have a health or medicine background, but our classroom examples will have that focus.

Structure

At CodeAbode, we believe that you learn by doing, especially when it comes to programming and designing mobile apps. We will dedicate lots of class time to actually programming – a lab environment if you will. Our instructors are coaches, coaches who are also developers and have a passion to teach as well. Our bootcamp schedule includes an hour-long midday break, where students can eat lunch and network. When you are at CodeAbode, we want you to learn by doing.


Each student can come with an app that you want to build – it can be a game, or a product idea, or a mobile version of software that you use at work. But we will also have apps that you can build based on our core mission of health and medicine. We have a fitness app for you to clone, we have biomedical visualization apps, and we also have a mobile analytics app based on data from an EHR system.


We will put everyone in 4-5 person teams so that you can learn from your fellow students. But everyone will have a different part to play so that we make sure that everyone is learning the techniques as deeply as possible.


Every class larger than 20 students will have assistant instructors as well as the lead instructor so that the coaching will be intense. We have a lot to teach in the 420 course hours, so we want every minute to count.


All classes will be taught at the National Museum of Health and Medicine Chicago, so at breaks you can mingle in the exhibits, network with the interns or staff of the Museum, as well as with your fellow classmates at the café, or in the auditorium space. Many students will stay after class to work on projects or discuss ideas with their classmates. The building is open 24/7, so we encourage you to get immersed.

Course 1: HTML5 and CSS3
Here you will learn how to set up a web site using the latest generation of HTML (hypertext markup language) and CSS (cascading style sheets). These are the foundational authoring tools for the web, supported by all modern web browsers. We will have sample web sites for you to author, and through the class you will begin to build a portfolio of your work. The basics are quite easy to master, and with our help you will be able to create dynamic web sites to show off your newly-learned skills.

Course 2: Phone Apps/Introduction to Programming
This course will focus on getting you ready to really program, by teaching the basics of programming using JavaScript. This is a language that much mobile development uses, especially for accessing databases that you will continue to learn and extend across the sequence. We will also be introducing some tools that allow you to create once, and publish apps across platforms. In addition to supercharging your portfolio right away and getting you excited about how fast you are creating meaningful apps that work both on the web and mobile devices, you will expand your understanding of many of the concepts you will master at a deeper level in future courses. At the end of this module, you’ll be able to publish on the AppStore and GooglePlay.

Course 3: Server-side Integration
This course is where you will start to focus on where many apps find their real value – integrating with existing data bases and being able to tap into the power of servers that can be located anywhere in the world. You got a taste of this already in the Mobile Web Apps course, but now we are going to add the dynamics of large data sets. The most widely used programming language for integrating remote data resources into mobile apps is JavaScript. You will learn how to access and control remote databases, how to selectively pull and update data, how to use APIs that are built into application software, and how to optimize the use of both proprietary and public databases over the network. This is a critical skill for working in a corporate IT department, and a powerful tool for building apps that have value in the real world.

Course 4: Advanced Programming/Introduction to Object-Oriented Programming
This course will extend the second class. Now that we have integrated the server-side, we are ready to teach you dynamic updating of your apps, client-side calculations and visualizations, extensions to the auto-creation of iOS and Android apps, and much more. In this course we will also introduce you to object-oriented programming concepts because you will need it to take full advantage of the native languages of the top two phone environments. The language we’ll introduce you to for OOP is Java.

Course 5: Phone-specific Programming
Note: For the bootcamp, each student can pick either Apple or Android to learn. We want to keep the number of languages you have to learn limited, so we are giving you an option of one or the other. If you want to learn both, you can take the other one at night.
Option 1: Native iOS - Swift
Having already built some Apple mobile apps in the Mobile Web Apps course, in this course you will dive in more deeply and learn to build fully-native iPhone and iPad apps using a new Apple tool called Swift. Swift is an easy-to-learn successor to the C and Objective C languages and generates superfast and efficient code to run on iPhones and iPads. You will learn to code in Swift and XCode, how they compare to HTML, and how to not only design apps, but also to make them dynamic, even using features of the devices like the accelerometer and sensors.
Option 2: Native Android - Java
Next you are going to learn how to extend your newly-learned native-app skills to the world of Android mobile devices, using the Java, programming language. In this course, we will use Java the Android Developer Tools, and the Integrated Development Environment (IDE) to develop native Android apps. You will build on what you learned in the previous courses, and the comparisons and contrasts will start coming faster as you do similar things in what will now be your fourth application environment. In this class however, we won’t just be recreating the same app again. We want to build your portfolio, so we’ll create new apps, and then you may even choose to take the challenge of converting them back to iOS as well.

Course 6: Advanced Server & Databases
Because server-side applications represent such a critical area for success in building mission-critical systems for health and medicine, this course will continue you on the path of mastering the server side of the equation. In this course you will learn how to use advanced tools such as Angular/JS, Node/JS, Mongo/DB and a variety of JavaScript add-ons to design, implement and deploy advanced applications that can leverage powerful server side resources.

Course 7: Advanced Health & Medical Apps
Because we focus on health, medicine, and fitness, we want you to be able to deal with unique challenges in this space – privacy, large data sets, huge image files, constant streaming of fitness data, etc. This course will have you learning advanced techniques to address these challenges. For example, you will use compression algorithms for large files, manipulate models in 3D space, assure authorization to only validated users of private data, interact with medical models, and design for the next generation of health and fitness device platforms including the Apple Watch.

Student Profile

High school students - Are you a gamer or an Instagram poster? Are you interested in technology and want to create your own game or application. Maybe you’d like to learn to design something new for the AppleWatch. Boost your college application or impress your friends. Maybe even start a company in your garage (or dining room).


College grads – Hey liberal arts, social science and hard science grads. Put your degree to work in a different way. Encapsulate it in an app that you know will make a difference. Alternatively, you think that coding will be a good place to be professionally (at least until grad school). Get trained and out there with a practical skill in 14 weeks.


IT professionals – Perhaps you are a database expert or a SharePoint programmer, or work in C++, but you’d also like to master Java, JavaScript, Objective C (and Apple’s new Swift), you’ll find a fast path to further mastery at CodeAbode.


MedTech entrepreneurs – If your idea includes anything in the broad fitness, health, medical education arena, there is no better place than CodeAbode to learn to program in the relevant languages using the relevant tools. Our instructors have been there, our examples will come from leading existing apps and innovative apps that we’ve done for the National Museum, and our network will enable you to move from app to start-up company.


Marketing managers – Are you constantly managing web, social media, or application projects, overseeing vendors (or internal departments) that help you with the technical work? Would you like to better master the technologies behind web and mobile apps? CodeAbode is for you.


Tech-savvy doctors – If you are the kind of doctor that is constantly tinkering, thinking about how to improve the software in the office, or thinking about to use an AppleWatch or Fitbit for patient compliance, or an app for patient education, come take a couple courses with us, and prototype your ideas.


Anyone who wants to learn to code – There is no one size fits all at CodeAbode. We want to unlock your potential. If you’ve always wanted to create a digital footprint, we want to help you do it. Maybe you’ve tried to take an on-line class, but it’s hard with no coach and no colleagues. Come try our supportive environment.

Features

1. Practice – See previous section. In class, we will focus on group and individual work, and you will be able to work on your projects while you are learning. Lots of in-class work time will allow you to complete assignments with the expert guidance of your instructors. That way we can coach you and you’ll emerge from the program with completed apps.

2. Health and Medicine focus – Chicago is the Silicon Valley of two related industries – medical equipment and application software for businesses. Chicago is also strong in hospitals, pharmaceutical companies, and many other health care related companies. Plus you will be working out of the only satellite of one of the oldest museums in the country (founded in 1865) and it focuses on health and medicine.

3. Employers – This means that our partners are the entire medical establishment of Chicago. You will study with some of their employees, and they are our recruiting partners, as well, if you want a full-time IT position in a prestigious firm.

4. Incubator and Funders – We have connections with the premier health informatics incubator, and with multiple venture capital funds. If your goal is to launch a product or company, CodeAbode is the right place to start.
